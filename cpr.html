CPR

CPR stands for cardiopulmonary resuscitation. It can be used during emergency situations when someone is either choking, has drowned, or is having a heart attack. CPR keeps oxygenated blood flowing to the brain and other organs while medical treatment is being prepared. If you are untrained, do 100 to 120 chest compressions per minute. If you are well trained, start with chest compressions followed by mouth to mouth resuscitation if needed.
